# Compliance — read before dialing on a coach's behalf

Not legal advice; have a lawyer review before you sell this at scale. But
these are the rules that get AI calling operations fined or shut down, and
your coaches will hold YOU responsible.

## TCPA (US federal) — the big one
- The FCC has confirmed AI-generated voices count as "artificial or
  prerecorded voice" under the TCPA. Outbound calls to cell phones with an
  AI voice require **prior express written consent** — a checkbox on the
  lead form saying they agree to receive calls, including automated/AI
  calls, at the number provided. Screenshot-log that consent per lead.
- Statutory damages are $500–$1,500 **per call**. This is the number to
  quote when a coach asks why the consent checkbox matters.
- Calling window: 8am–9pm in the lead's local time. Enforce in the dialer.
- Honor opt-outs immediately and permanently (`mark_do_not_contact` exists
  for this) and scrub against the National DNC Registry where applicable.

## State AI-disclosure laws
- A growing set of states (California among them) require disclosing that
  the caller is an AI/bot. The safe, simple policy: **always disclose in
  the first sentence** ("...this is Riley, Coach Marcus's AI assistant").
  In practice it costs very little in booking rate and removes a whole
  category of risk and bad press.

## Call recording
- Two-party-consent states (CA, FL, IL, PA, WA, and others) require all
  parties to be notified of recording. If you record calls for QA (you
  should), add a notice to the greeting or the pre-call SMS.

## Claims & earnings
- The agent's prompt already bans outcome guarantees and invented
  testimonials. Keep it that way — FTC treats AI-spoken claims exactly
  like human ones, and coaching is an FTC-watched vertical (earnings
  claims especially, for business coaches).

## Positioning that keeps you safest (and sells better anyway)
1. **Inbound-triggered calls first**: speed-to-lead callbacks within
   minutes of a form fill. Consent is fresh, answer rates are 3–5x, and
   compliance risk is minimal. Lead with this offer.
2. Cold outbound to purchased lists with an AI voice: **do not offer this.**
   It's the TCPA lawsuit shape.
3. Reactivation of a coach's old lead list: only if those leads originally
   gave written phone consent that covers automated calls.
